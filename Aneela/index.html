Burning

Curriculum Design & Innovation

Sample Unit: Biochemistry & Genetics (Blended Delivery)

Unit Overview

This unit introduces core principles of biochemistry and genetics with practical application and interdisciplinary connection. Students explore biomolecules, metabolic pathways, gene expression, and inheritance through a blended approach using theory, virtual labs, case-based learning, and collaborative projects.

Canvas LMS Virtual labs Collaborative projects

Learning Outcomes

Students will be able to:

1) Explain structures and functions of key biomolecules in cellular processes.
2) Describe core genetic mechanisms (replication, transcription, translation, inheritance).
3) Analyse biochemical/genetic data using virtual and simulated labs.
4) Collaborate in teams to research and present contemporary issues.
5) Reflect on ethical, social, and health-related implications of technologies.

Assessment Design

Assessment aligns with learning outcomes through quizzes (formative), virtual lab reports (analysis and interpretation), a team research project (science communication), and a reflective journal (ethical/social/health connections).

Tools & Learning Technology

Canvas LMS for delivery and assessment, supported by virtual lab tools and curated readings/media. Students also receive structured support through drop-in sessions, forums, and guided study opportunities.

Student Feedback & Reflection

Feedback → Action → Impact

Strengths (Common Themes)

Students consistently highlight clarity, approachability, and supportive check-ins. I use this feedback to sustain strong formative practices and inclusive teaching routines.

“You are very friendly and helpful, you make sure we understand the lesson.”

Improvements Implemented

When students requested clearer slides and broader participation, I reduced text density, increased visual explanations, added guided notes, and introduced Think–Pair–Share plus inclusive questioning strategies.

Reflective Summary

Feedback is treated as data. I analyse patterns across cohorts, trial targeted changes, and re-check impact through engagement, confidence, and performance indicators. This cycle supports continuous improvement and underpins my SoTL approach.

Scholarship of Teaching & Learning

SoTL Projects (2024–2025)

UDL Readiness Mapping

Mixed-methods evaluation of how teachers integrate Universal Design for Learning into planning. Findings identified barriers/enablers and informed a UDL readiness self-assessment tool used in professional development contexts.

Equity in Collaborative Work

Structured group roles (Technician, Recorder, Calculator, Spokesperson) were introduced to promote equitable participation in lab settings. Rotation and choice improved buy-in and reduced dominance by high-achieving students.

Embedding LLN in Science Labs

Scaffolded language, literacy, and numeracy supports improved accessibility for EAL learners without reducing scientific rigor. Plain-language protocols and visual guides strengthened safety and confidence.

AI & Creativity in Science Education

Developed a structured “AI-mediated creativity” model integrating prompt engineering, ethical AI use, and scaffolded creative tasks. Presented within a higher education innovation context, with follow-up evaluation planned.
